Still In Bloom

Length: Feature, approx. 90 mins
Shooting Format: RED Weapon, 8K resolution
Writer/Director: Christopher Carson Emmons
Producer: Ramesh Anthony
Cinematographer: Nathaniel Haban

Synopsis

Artistically inclined Lily struggles to make it professionally as a mime, to the chagrin of her single father, Miles, who’s just been laid off. Miles’ girlfriend, Margot, agrees to employ a reluctant Lily part time at her company to help with the bills . Lily’s disapproval of Margot causes a rift between her and her father, and his desire for Margot to move in with them further complicating the dynamic. While miming on the street corner one day for tips, Lily is approached by a documentary filmmaker named Leah, who proposes the concept of creating a documentary about her. Lily accepts, and before long falls for Leah in` the process of creating the film. Their personal relationship deepens, complicating their working relationship. One night, Lily walks in on Leah with a boy, then goes home feeling betrayed, only to hear from Margot that Miles has just died in a freak accident. As Lily’s world falls apart and she struggles to pick up the pieces, Margot steps up and tries to fill the role of mother for her deceased partner’s troubled daughter. Margot suggests she would be a good agent for Lily, and begins booking appearances for her. Lily is conflicted as she tries to make her mark as an artist, but is grateful for Margot’s support. Margot soon books a tour in Paris, a longtime dream of Lily’s, just as she begins reconnecting with Leah, causing her to have to choose what’s most important to her in life, flowering love or her blossoming career.

Producer's Statement

I was instantly drawn to the subtlety and truthfulness of the characters when I first read the screenplay . It was right then when I made it a resolve to take the story universal through cinema

In “Still to Bloom”, Lily’s life unfolds and blooms in front of us, and also through the lens of Leah, a filmmaker who is captivated by the dying art form called mime . We also learn about Lily’s admiration for her father, her fondness for Ella Fitzgerald’s “Goodnight My Love”, and the simplicity in life that she seeks . While her only outlet is through her mime acts .. and it’s a world in which nothing else matters, as in life .. circumstances force her to adopt to an evolving world, and to accept the realities of her relationships . Lily is a homage to all of us who aspire to be true to ourselves . The choices she makes define who she is even if it comes at the cost of being irrelevant to the people she mimes for on the streets

The concrete details of Lily’s life, her struggles, and all the real humor gives the film an authenticity that I hope will resonate and make it emotional for all kinds of people . While this story is distinct, I do not intend to approach it as a “small” film or a “quirky” film or even an “indie” film . I only get to tell this story once, so I want it to be bighearted, for a big audience, progressive and innovative, and like Lily – deeply wanting to connect with people through her art . This is a story for you
